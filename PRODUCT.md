# PRODUCT.md — dhruvgairola.com

## Register

**Brand.** This is a personal blog. The design is the product.

## Who writes here

Dhruv Gairola. Engineering Manager at Okta (Privileged Access Management). Previously EM at Wealthsimple during its 8x scale-up to ~$50B AUM. MSc CS from McMaster (Data Science Research Lab, 3 ACM publications). Writes about engineering management, AI in software, security, algorithms, and occasional side-quests (lists of albums, movies, coffee).

## Who reads

Senior engineers and managers considering substantive essays. People who will read 2,000 words if the writing is trustworthy. Not skimmers.

## Voice

First-person, unshowy, senior. Concrete examples over abstractions. Mixes theory (papers, systems) with direct professional experience. Never hype-y; never corporate. Occasionally dry.

## Strategic principles

1. **Reading comes first.** Every design decision must protect the reader's attention. If it competes with the prose, cut it.
2. **Committed, not safe.** The old site defaulted to a Jekyll template. The new one must make choices — in color, in type, in layout — that a reader will remember.
3. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Hierarchy through scale and weight, not decoration.
4. **No category reflex.** Engineering/management content does *not* require a cool-gray SaaS aesthetic. This is an essayist's blog, not a product dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Medium / Substack defaults — indistinct
- Literal Geist / Vercel clone — training-data reflex for "engineer blog"
- Dev.to / Hashnode — aggressive density, generic cards
- Dark-mode-only "engineer-coded" sites — category reflex

## Positive references

Linear blog, Vercel blog, Rauno's site — for restraint and typographic confidence. The goal is that *energy*, expressed through a distinct warm palette and serif accents rather than cloned tokens.

## Tone anti-patterns (in design and copy)

- No em dashes in copy
- No "AI/ML-powered" language
- No cliché metric heroes
- No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no side-stripe callouts
